## How to use this content

Each entry below is the **base interpretation for that pair**. In the app, a one-line introduction precedes it, varying by aspect type:

- **Conjunction ☌** — *"A conjunction fuses these two principles into a single, undivided force."*
- **Sextile ⚹** — *"A sextile creates cooperative opportunity between these two principles."*
- **Square □** — *"A square creates tension and friction between these two principles that demands resolution."*
- **Trine △** — *"A trine connects these two principles with ease and natural flow."*
- **Opposition ☍** — *"An opposition places these two principles in direct confrontation across the chart."*

# PLANETARY PAIRS (21)

---

**☉ SUN — ☽ MOON**

The two luminaries in combination unite the principles of solar authority and lunar popular connection into a single powerful force. The native tends toward high public standing, association with persons of rank and power, and a capacity for both command and popularity that is rarely found together. Valens notes that this combination makes men successful in business enterprises, leaders of cities, gifted with a very high public image, munificent, and governing. The challenge is that the lunar principle introduces an element of changeability — the good fortune this combination produces does not always endure.

*"The Sun and the Moon are good — they are productive of associations with the great and of high rank, as well as possession of estates, property, money, and adornment."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☉ SUN — ☿ MERCURY**

The Sun and Mercury in combination unite the principles of solar authority and mercurial intelligence in the same sphere of the chart. Mercury is never far from the Sun in any nativity, and their combination tends to produce a native of adaptable, many-sided intelligence — flexible, self-controlled, and at home in public life. Valens describes this combination as making men pure, sensible, of good judgment, lovers of beauty, learned, and beneficent. There is, however, a tendency toward ups and downs in livelihood, as the mercurial nature introduces instability into the solar desire for enduring status.

*"Mercury and the Sun make adaptable men with many friends — pure, sensible men, men of good judgment, lovers of beauty, learned men, initiates into divine matters, beneficent, fond of their associates."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☉ SUN — ♀ VENUS**

The Sun and Venus in combination unite the principles of solar authority and Venusian beauty, pleasure, and grace. The native tends toward a genuinely charming and socially gifted character, with a natural capacity for attracting both recognition and affection. Valens describes this combination as harmonious and glorious — making men successful in their enterprises, causing the association of male and female, and bringing conveyances and material support. There may be difficulty with respect to wife or children, particularly when Venus is setting.

*"Venus and the Sun are in harmony, glorious, bestowers of good — they cause the association of male and female, they bring conveyances, and make men successful in their enterprises."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☉ SUN — ♂ MARS**

The Sun and Mars in combination unite the principles of solar authority and martial force — two hot, active, and masculine energies that intensify each other. The native tends toward boldness, ambition, and a restless drive for preeminence that can produce either great achievement or great conflict depending on how the energy is directed. Ptolemy notes that the Sun and Mars together produce effects that are forceful, direct, and capable of either impressive leadership or destructive excess. The challenge is that solar pride and martial combativeness can work against each other when circumstances call for patience or diplomacy.

*"The nature of Mars is chiefly to dry and to burn — and the Sun, which is hot and drying, intensifies this quality in combination, producing men of great force and equally great risk."*
— Claudius Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos, Book I

---

**☉ SUN — ♃ JUPITER**

The Sun and Jupiter in combination unite the two great benefic and authoritative forces in the traditional system — the Sun as the principle of kingly authority and Jupiter as the principle of wisdom, justice, and abundance. Valens describes this combination as producing distinguished men, rulers, governors, dictators, vigorous men, honored and blessed by the crowd — wealthy, rich, living with much spectacle. This is among the most powerful combinations for public success and lasting recognition. The challenge is a tendency toward showiness and a risk of overstating one's achievements when Jupiter is setting.

*"Jupiter and the Sun together produce distinguished men, rulers, governors, dictators, vigorous men, honored and blessed by the crowd — wealthy, rich, living with much spectacle."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☉ SUN — ♄ SATURN**

The Sun and Saturn in combination place the principles of solar authority and Saturnine restriction in direct relationship — and Valens describes this combination as fundamentally at odds. The native may find that recognition and authority are given and taken away, that great persons become secret enemies, and that the solar drive for public prominence is repeatedly undermined by Saturnine forces of delay and opposition. Yet the native is described as self-controlled in the onslaught of reversals, not without resources, and ultimately outliving most of their enemies.

*"Saturn and the Sun are at odds, giving and taking away possessions and friendships maliciously — those born under such a juncture suffer secret enmities and threats from great persons, but they are self-controlled and long-suffering."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☽ MOON — ☿ MERCURY**

The Moon and Mercury in combination unite the principles of lunar popular connection and mercurial intelligence in the same sphere. Valens describes this as a good combination with respect to the union and status of men and women, the power of speech and education, and commerce. The native tends toward resourcefulness, inquisitiveness, and a natural gift for communication that resonates with a broad public. There is, however, a changeability to the native's activities and intentions — the mercurial tendency toward variety combines with the lunar tendency toward flux, producing a native who is noble in adversity but subject to instability.

*"Mercury and the Moon are good with respect to the union and status of men and women, with respect to the power of speech and education, and concerning commerce — they make men who are resourceful, experienced, and inquisitive."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☽ MOON — ♀ VENUS**

The Moon and Venus in combination unite the two feminine, nocturnal, and moist planets of the traditional system. Valens describes this combination as good with respect to rank, acquisitions, and the inception of business, but unsteady with respect to living together, friendships, and marriage — bringing rivalry and hostility as well as ill-treatment from relatives and friends. The native has a genuine capacity for beauty, social grace, and emotional responsiveness, but the combination of two mutable, relationship-oriented principles can produce instability in the very sphere where both planets most naturally seek expression.

*"Venus and the Moon are good with respect to rank, acquisitions, and the inception of business, but they are unsteady with respect to living together, friendships, and marriage — bringing rivalry and hostility."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☽ MOON — ♂ MARS**

The Moon and Mars in combination place the principles of lunar nurture and emotional sensitivity in direct contact with martial force and heat. This is one of the more challenging combinations in the traditional system — the Moon's need for nourishment and popular connection is fundamentally at odds with Mars's drying and burning nature. The native may experience a deep tension between emotional vulnerability and the impulse toward aggression, or between the desire for comfort and the restless need for action. When this combination operates constructively, it can produce a native of formidable emotional courage.

*"The nature of Mars is chiefly to dry and to burn — and the Moon, which is primarily moist and cooling, is placed under severe strain in combination with the martial fire."*
— Claudius Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos, Book I

---

**☽ MOON — ♃ JUPITER**

The Moon and Jupiter in combination unite the two principles most associated with abundance, popular connection, and genuine benefit in the traditional system. Valens describes this as a good combination — acquisitive, causing men to be masters of adornments and slaves, bestowing distinguished offices and ranks, causing men to benefit from women and distinguished individuals, and making those who are trusted and who find treasures. The native tends toward genuine good fortune, particularly in dealings with the public and with women, and there is a natural expansiveness and generosity to the emotional life.

*"Jupiter and the Moon are good, acquisitive — they cause men to be masters of adornments, to be thought worthy of gifts and honors — they make treasurers, men who lend much, who are trusted, and who find treasures."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☽ MOON — ♄ SATURN**

The Moon and Saturn in combination place the principles of lunar nourishment and popular connection in relationship with the cold, restricting, and melancholic influence of Saturn. Valens describes this combination as beneficial for material acquisition — productive of money, estates, ship ownership, and profits from the deceased — but deeply unstable with respect to women, producing insecurity and pain because of separations, hatred, and grief. The native may accumulate genuine material resources while experiencing profound difficulty in the personal and emotional sphere that the Moon most naturally governs.

*"Saturn and the Moon are beneficial with respect to money, estates, and profits from the deceased — but this combination is unsteady with respect to women: insecure and painful because of separations, hatred, and grief."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☿ MERCURY — ♀ VENUS**

Mercury and Venus in combination unite the principles of mercurial intelligence and Venusian grace — both temperate, sociable, and oriented toward the pleasures of culture and exchange. Valens describes this combination as harmonious — making men sociable and gracious, gregarious and hedonistic, paying attention to education and sensibility, receiving honors and gifts. The native tends toward a genuine aesthetic and intellectual refinement, a wide social circle, and an easy facility in cultural and commercial life. There is, however, a changeability in agreements with women that Valens notes as characteristic of this combination.

*"Venus and Mercury are in harmony — they make men sociable and gracious, gregarious and hedonistic, paying attention to education and sensibility, receiving honors and gifts."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☿ MERCURY — ♂ MARS**

Mercury and Mars in combination place the principles of mercurial intelligence in contact with martial force and aggression. Valens describes this combination as not good — causing hostility, lawsuits, reversals, malice, and betrayals, as well as wrongs from superiors or inferiors. There is a tendency toward forgery, embezzlement, and the accumulation of debt and infamy. When the combination operates constructively, however, it produces men who are athletic, martial, commanding, and inquisitive of the occult — the sharpness of Mars giving Mercury's intelligence a cutting and investigative quality.

*"Mercury and Mars are not good — they cause hostility, lawsuits, reversals, malice, betrayals, wrongs from superiors or inferiors. These stars make some men athletic, martial, commanding, inquisitive of the occult."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☿ MERCURY — ♃ JUPITER**

Mercury and Jupiter in combination unite the principles of mercurial intelligence and Jovian wisdom in the same sphere — an alliance of the two planets most associated with knowledge, communication, and the management of affairs. Valens describes this combination as good, harmonious, and supervisory — making men who are managers, overseers of affairs, in posts of trust and administration, successful as secretaries and accountants, and respected in education. The native tends toward genuine intellectual authority, a facility for financial and administrative management, and a capacity for inspiring trust in others.

*"Jupiter and Mercury are good, in harmony, and supervisory — they make men who are managers, overseers of affairs, in posts of trust and administration, successful as secretaries and accountants, and respected in education."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☿ MERCURY — ♄ SATURN**

Mercury and Saturn in combination unite the principles of mercurial intelligence and Saturnine restriction and depth. Valens describes this as an alliance that is productive of activities but that brings slanders about religion, lawsuits, and debts, as well as disturbances about written matters and money. However, these stars make men who are not without resources and not unintelligent — with much experience and awareness, curious, far-seeing scholars, seekers after mystic lore, and reverers of the gods. The native tends toward a deep and investigative intelligence that operates most naturally in the hidden dimensions of knowledge.

*"Saturn and Mercury are allies and productive of activities — they make men who are not without resources, not unintelligent, with much experience and awareness, curious, far-seeing scholars, seekers after mystic lore."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♀ VENUS — ♂ MARS**

Venus and Mars in combination place the two principles most associated with desire and conflict in direct relationship — and Valens describes them as fundamentally at odds. This combination makes men unsteady and weak of mind, causes rivalry and potential for violence, and brings association with persons who are blameworthy and fickle. The native may be prone to passionate and unstable relationships, a tendency toward jealousy and possessiveness, and the risk of being wronged by or because of women. When this combination operates constructively, it produces a native of intense magnetism and a powerful, if turbulent, creative and erotic energy.

*"Venus and Mars are at odds — they make men unsteady and weak of mind; they cause rivalry; they cause men to be malicious and plotters, and to suffer crises, upsets, and debts because of women."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♀ VENUS — ♃ JUPITER**

Venus and Jupiter in combination unite the two traditional benefics — the two planets most associated with abundance, pleasure, and genuine good fortune. Valens describes this combination as good, in harmony, and productive of rank and profits — bringing new acquisitions, gifts, adornments, control over slaves, rulerships, the begetting of children, high priesthoods, and preeminence among the masses. The native tends toward a genuinely fortunate and pleasurable life, with real social and material gifts that come with relative ease. The only shadow Valens notes is a tendency toward ups and downs with respect to marriages and children.

*"Jupiter and Venus are good, in harmony, productive of rank and profits — bringing new acquisitions, gifts, adornments, high priesthoods, preeminence among the masses, honors of garlands and gold crowns."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♀ VENUS — ♄ SATURN**

Venus and Saturn in combination unite the principles of Venusian pleasure and beauty with Saturnine restriction and endurance. Valens describes this combination as harmonious with respect to activities and employment — promoting success with respect to entanglements and marriage, agreeing and beneficial only for a time, not to the end. There is a tendency toward abuse, divorces, and inconstancy, and a risk of entanglement with the base-born. When this combination operates constructively, it produces a native whose experience of love is disciplined and enduring — beauty appreciated through the lens of time and necessity.

*"Saturn and Venus act harmoniously with respect to activities and employment — they promote success with respect to entanglements and marriage, agreeing and beneficial only for a time, not to the end — causing abuse, divorces, and inconstancy."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♂ MARS — ♃ JUPITER**

Mars and Jupiter in combination unite the principles of martial force and Jovian abundance — two expansive, masculine, and active energies that amplify each other's capacity for public achievement. Valens describes this combination as making glorious and showy characters, friends of the great or of kings, distinguished governors and receivers of stipends, those making a career in public affairs or in campaigns, and those considered worthy of honor and status. The challenge is an uncertainty in livelihood and a tendency to toss away possessions — the Jovian expansiveness and the Martian recklessness reinforcing each other's excesses.

*"Jupiter and Mars make glorious and showy characters, friends of the great or of kings, distinguished governors, those making a career in public affairs or in campaigns — considered worthy of honor and status, but uncertain in their livelihoods."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♂ MARS — ♄ SATURN**

Mars and Saturn in combination place the two malefics of the traditional system in direct relationship — and Valens describes this as hostile and productive of reversals and ruin, bringing family quarrels, disharmony, and hatred, along with treachery, plots, malevolence, and trials. This is among the most challenging combinations in the traditional system. However, Valens notes that if these stars are not in their own or in operative signs, and if they have benefics in aspect, they produce distinguished and noble nativities — although unsteady in their happiness and prone to unexpected dangers and treachery.

*"Saturn and Mars are hostile, productive of reversals and ruin — they bring family quarrels, disharmony, and hatred, along with treachery, plots, malevolence, and trials."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♃ JUPITER — ♄ SATURN**

Jupiter and Saturn in combination unite the two slowest-moving planets of the traditional system — and Valens describes this as a combination in agreement, bringing about benefits from legacies and adoptions, and causing men to be masters of property consisting of land, to be guardians, managers of others' property, stewards, and tax gatherers. The native tends toward a serious, disciplined, and administratively gifted character — someone who manages what has been inherited or entrusted to them with patience and endurance. This combination is characteristic of persons who rise to genuine authority through long effort rather than sudden fortune.

*"Saturn and Jupiter are in agreement with each other — they bring about benefits from legacies and adoptions, and they cause men to be masters of property, to be guardians, managers of others' property, stewards, and tax gatherers."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

# PLANETS + ASCENDANT (7)

---

**☉ SUN — ASC**

The Sun in aspect with the Ascendant places the solar principle of authority, identity, and recognition in direct relationship with the native's physical person and outward bearing. The native's sense of self is strongly solar — oriented toward dignity, prominence, and the desire to be seen and acknowledged as a person of worth and authority. When harmonious, this aspect produces a native of natural confidence and commanding presence. When tense, the solar drive for recognition can create friction with the circumstances of the native's immediate environment and physical constitution.

*"The sun indicates kingship, rule, intellect, intelligence, beauty, motion, loftiness of fortune — and at the Ascendant, these qualities are stamped upon the very body and bearing of the native."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☽ MOON — ASC**

The Moon in aspect with the Ascendant connects the lunar principle of emotional responsiveness, popular connection, and bodily sensitivity to the native's outward identity. The native tends to be deeply impressionable, emotionally visible, and naturally attuned to the moods of those around them. There is often a popular quality to the personal bearing — an ability to reflect the needs and expectations of others that draws sympathy and connection. The body and constitution tend toward the lunar — changeable, sensitive, and closely tied to emotional states.

*"The Moon is the governor of the moist element — and at the Ascendant, its influence shapes the native's constitution, bearing, and immediate responsiveness to the world around them."*
— Claudius Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos, Book I

---

**☿ MERCURY — ASC**

Mercury in aspect with the Ascendant connects the mercurial principle of intelligence, communication, and mental agility to the native's outward identity and personal bearing. The native is perceived by others first and foremost through the quality of their mind — their quickness, their articulateness, and their capacity for intellectual engagement. There is a natural restlessness and versatility to the personal bearing, and the native tends to be known in their environment as someone who thinks quickly and communicates with ease.

*"Mercury indicates education, letters, disputation, reasoning, interpretation — and at the Ascendant, these mercurial qualities become the primary lens through which the native is known to others."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♀ VENUS — ASC**

Venus in aspect with the Ascendant connects the Venusian principles of beauty, grace, and social charm to the native's outward identity and physical appearance. The native tends to be perceived as attractive, pleasant, and naturally gracious — someone whose bearing invites affection and ease. Valens associates Venus in the Ascendant with great good fortune and renown, and an aspect to the Ascendant carries similar themes: the native tends to make favorable impressions, attract social support, and move through their environment with a natural Venusian ease.

*"Venus, when allotted the hour or the Lot and in the Ascendant, indicates great good and makes men far-renowned — and in aspect, her gracious influence touches the native's bearing and the impression they make on others."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book II

---

**♂ MARS — ASC**

Mars in aspect with the Ascendant connects the martial principles of energy, force, and combativeness to the native's outward identity and physical constitution. The native tends to be perceived as direct, vigorous, and physically assertive — someone who engages with their environment through initiative and action rather than through patience or diplomacy. The body tends toward a martial quality — active, heat-prone, and prone to the injuries and fevers that Valens associates with Mars. When harmonious, this aspect produces a native of genuine courage and physical energy.

*"Mars indicates force, violence, and commands — and at the Ascendant, the martial principle is written into the native's very body and bearing, making them known for their energy and their direct engagement with the world."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♃ JUPITER — ASC**

Jupiter in aspect with the Ascendant connects the Jovian principles of wisdom, abundance, and beneficent authority to the native's outward identity and personal bearing. The native tends to be perceived as noble, generous, and worthy of trust — someone whose presence inspires confidence and goodwill. Valens associates Jupiter in the Ascendant with great fortune from youth, and an aspect to the Ascendant carries similar themes: the native's circumstances tend to support rather than obstruct, and others tend to extend genuine goodwill toward them.

*"Jupiter, when allotted the hour or the Lot and in the Ascendant, causes men to be very fortunate from youth — and in aspect, its beneficent influence touches the native's bearing and the fortune that attaches to their person."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book II

---

**♄ SATURN — ASC**

Saturn in aspect with the Ascendant connects the Saturnine principles of restriction, gravity, and endurance to the native's outward identity and physical constitution. The native tends to be perceived as serious, reserved, and somewhat burdensome — someone who carries the weight of responsibility visibly. The body tends toward the Saturnine — lean, dark, and resilient through endurance rather than through natural vitality. When harmonious, this aspect produces a native of genuine authority built through sustained effort and a natural inclination toward the long view.

*"Saturn makes men care-worn, self-depreciating, solitary, downcast, miserable — and at the Ascendant, these qualities shape the native's constitution and the gravity of their personal bearing."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

# PLANETS + DESCENDANT (7)

---

**☉ SUN — DSC**

The Sun in aspect with the Descendant places the solar principle in the sphere of partnership, marriage, and the native's significant others. The native tends toward partners who are solar in character — authoritative, prominent, or highly visible — and the native's own solar ambitions are significantly shaped by the quality of their closest relationships. There is often a seeking of recognition through partnership rather than through solitary achievement, and the native's identity is in part defined by who they choose to unite with.

*"The sun indicates kingship, authority, and loftiness of fortune — and at the Descendant, these solar qualities are sought in and through the native's partners and significant others."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☽ MOON — DSC**

The Moon in aspect with the Descendant connects the lunar principle of emotional responsiveness and popular connection to the sphere of partnership and marriage. The native tends toward partners who are lunar in character — emotionally sensitive, popular, changeable, or connected to the public sphere — and close relationships are characterized by a deep emotional attunement and a natural mirroring quality. There may be a tendency toward relationships that are nourishing but unstable, reflecting the Moon's own changeable nature.

*"The Moon governs the moist and changeable element — and at the Descendant, these lunar qualities shape the partners the native attracts and the emotional texture of their closest bonds."*
— Claudius Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos, Book I

---

**☿ MERCURY — DSC**

Mercury in aspect with the Descendant connects the mercurial principle of intelligence and communication to the sphere of partnership and marriage. The native tends toward partners who are mercurial in character — intelligent, articulate, versatile, or commercially gifted — and close relationships are characterized by intellectual exchange and communicative engagement. There is often a preference for partnerships that are stimulating rather than merely comfortable, and a tendency to seek in others the mental quickness and adaptability the native values in themselves.

*"Mercury indicates education, letters, disputation, and reasoning — and at the Descendant, these mercurial qualities define the partners the native seeks and the intellectual character of their closest bonds."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♀ VENUS — DSC**

Venus in aspect with the Descendant connects the Venusian principles of beauty, grace, and pleasure to the sphere of partnership and marriage — the house most naturally associated with Venus in any case. The native tends toward partners who are Venusian in character — beautiful, artistic, socially gifted, or associated with pleasure and comfort — and close relationships are characterized by genuine affection and a mutual appreciation for beauty and harmony. This is one of the most favorable aspects for the quality of partnership and marriage.

*"Venus indicates desire, love, marriages, and the acquisition of beauty — and at the Descendant, these Venusian qualities define the partners the native attracts and the pleasurable character of their closest unions."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♂ MARS — DSC**

Mars in aspect with the Descendant places martial force and combativeness in the sphere of partnership and open enmity. The native tends toward partners who are martial in character — direct, energetic, and forceful — and close relationships may be characterized by passion, competition, or outright conflict. The seventh house governs both partners and open enemies, and Mars here can indicate that the native's most significant encounters with others — whether in love or in opposition — are characterized by a martial intensity that is rarely comfortable but seldom dull.

*"Mars indicates force, violence, and combativeness — and at the Descendant, these martial qualities define both the partners the native attracts and the open enemies they encounter in the course of life."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♃ JUPITER — DSC**

Jupiter in aspect with the Descendant connects the Jovian principles of wisdom, abundance, and beneficent authority to the sphere of partnership and marriage. The native tends toward partners who are Jovian in character — wise, generous, socially prominent, or associated with law, philosophy, or religion — and close relationships tend to be genuinely fortunate and expansive. Valens notes that benefics in the seventh house and ruling the Ascendant or Lot of Fortune indicate inheritances, sudden acquisition of property, and benefits that come through the unions of fortunate persons.

*"Benefics in this Place and ruling the Ascendant or the Lot of Fortune indicate good things for the native: inheritances, sudden acquisition of property, and benefits that come through the unions of fortunate persons."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book II

---

**♄ SATURN — DSC**

Saturn in aspect with the Descendant connects the Saturnine principles of restriction, gravity, and endurance to the sphere of partnership and marriage. The native tends toward partners who are Saturnine in character — older, more serious, more burdened, or associated with authority and long-term commitment — and close relationships may be characterized by duty and obligation rather than by ease and pleasure. There is often a quality of genuine permanence to the partnerships this aspect produces, built on real commitment rather than on romantic feeling alone.

*"Saturn makes men care-worn, solitary, and downcast — and at the Descendant, these Saturnine qualities define the serious and often burdened character of the partners the native attracts."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

# PLANETS + MIDHEAVEN (7)

---

**☉ SUN — MC**

The Sun in aspect with the Midheaven connects the solar principle of authority and recognition directly to the native's career, public reputation, and action in the world. This is one of the most powerful aspects for public life — the Midheaven governs the native's highest achievements and most visible role in society, and the Sun there amplifies the desire for and capacity to achieve genuine recognition. The native tends toward careers in positions of authority and toward a public reputation that is consistently solar — dignified, prominent, and oriented toward command.

*"The sun indicates kingship, rule, intellect, and loftiness of fortune — and at the Midheaven, the solar principle is at its most publicly expressed, governing the native's highest achievements and most visible role in the world."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**☽ MOON — MC**

The Moon in aspect with the Midheaven connects the lunar principle of popular connection and emotional responsiveness to the native's career and public reputation. The native tends toward careers that involve the public directly — food, care, the management of popular affairs, or any field where the native is seen and known by many. Public reputation is closely tied to popular sentiment, and the native's standing in the world tends to wax and wane with the cycles of public mood rather than remaining fixed and stable.

*"The ruler of the Midheaven, if situated favorably, makes vigorous and successful men — and the Moon at the Midheaven produces those known by name in many places for their popular appeal and their attunement to the needs of the masses."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book II

---

**☿ MERCURY — MC**

Mercury in aspect with the Midheaven connects the mercurial principles of intelligence, communication, and the management of information to the native's career and public reputation. The native tends toward careers built on communicative skill — writing, teaching, law, commerce, administration, or any field where the quality of the mind and the power of words are the primary tools of achievement. Public reputation is built through intellectual distinction and the respect of those who value clarity and knowledge.

*"Mercury indicates education, letters, disputation, reasoning, accounts, and communication — and at the Midheaven, these mercurial qualities govern the native's career and the public reputation they build through the exercise of intelligence."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♀ VENUS — MC**

Venus in aspect with the Midheaven connects the Venusian principles of beauty, grace, and social charm to the native's career and public reputation. The native tends toward careers in the arts, beauty, diplomacy, social administration, or any field where aesthetic judgment and gracious dealing are the primary tools of achievement. Public reputation is characterized by the native's capacity to create pleasure and beauty for others, and there is often genuine recognition and advancement through Venusian means.

*"Venus indicates desire, love, cheerfulness, friendship, beauty, and the acquisition of fine things — and at the Midheaven, these Venusian qualities govern the native's public role and the reputation they build through grace and the cultivation of beauty."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♂ MARS — MC**

Mars in aspect with the Midheaven connects the martial principles of force, courage, and decisive action to the native's career and public reputation. The native tends toward careers built on martial qualities — military service, surgery, competitive business, athletics, or any field requiring physical courage and decisive engagement. Public reputation is characterized by energy, force, and a capacity for bold action that others may admire or fear. Ptolemy associates the Midheaven with action and career, and Mars there produces persons known for their active and forceful engagement with the world.

*"Mars indicates force, wars, commands, campaigns and leadership — and at the Midheaven, the martial principle governs the native's public role and the reputation they build through courage, force, and decisive action."*
— Vettius Valens, Anthologies, Book I

---

**♃ JUPITER — MC**

Jupiter in aspect with the Midheaven connects the Jovian principles of wisdom, abundance, and beneficent authority to the native's career and public reputation. This is among the most favorable aspects for public life — the native tends toward positions of genuine authority and public trust, often in fields connected to Jupiter: law, philosophy, religion, governance, or the administration of institutions. Public reputation is characterized by wisdom, justice, and a genuine generosity of spirit that earns lasting and well-deserved recognition.
